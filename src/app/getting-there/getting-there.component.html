Getting There

✦

From the airport to El Poblado — everything you need to arrive with ease.

Your Arrival

José María Córdova International Airport

📍 Rionegro, Antioquia · Airport Code: MDE

Medellín's main international airport is located about 45 km east of the city in Rionegro. Most international flights — including connections through Bogotá, Miami, New York, and Madrid — arrive here. The drive to El Poblado takes roughly 45–60 minutes depending on traffic.

Recommended · Easiest Option

Uber or InDriver

The easiest and most comfortable way to get from the airport to El Poblado. Open Uber or InDriver once you land and request a ride directly to your hotel or Airbnb.

  • 🕐 ~45–60 minutes to El Poblado
  • 💵 ~$80,000–$150,000 COP (approx. $22–41 USD) — higher during peak hours or surge pricing
  • 📱 Download the app before you travel — both work well in Medellín

Tip: InDriver often has lower fares than Uber and lets you negotiate the price.

Option

Official Airport Taxi

Official yellow taxis are available just outside arrivals. Look for the official taxi booth inside the terminal to get a fixed-rate ticket before heading to the taxi stand — this avoids overcharging.

  • 🕐 ~45–60 minutes to El Poblado
  • 💵 ~$140,000–$200,000 COP (approx. $35–50 USD) fixed rate
  • 📍 Pick up at the official taxi stand outside arrivals

Once You Arrive

Getting Around El Poblado

El Poblado is one of Medellín's most walkable and tourist-friendly neighborhoods. Most hotels, restaurants, and attractions are within easy walking distance of Parque El Poblado.

  • 🚇 Metro: El Poblado station on Línea A connects you to the rest of the city
  • 🚗 Uber / InDriver: Readily available throughout the neighborhood
  • 🚶 Walking: Most of the action is within a 10–15 minute walk

Helpful Tips

Before You Go

  • 💱 Exchange some cash at the airport or use a local ATM — many smaller vendors prefer COP (Colombian Pesos). As of April 2026, $1 USD ≈ $3,678 COP.
  • 🌤️ September averages a daytime high of 25°C (77°F) and a nighttime low of 16°C (62°F) — light layers for evenings and a compact umbrella are recommended as showers are frequent but short-lived.
  • 🛂 US, EU, and most passport holders do not need a visa for Colombia for stays under 90 days.
